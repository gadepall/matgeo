\item The matrix 
	$
\vec{A} = \myvec{
\frac{3}{2} & 0 & \frac{1}{2} \\
0 & -1 & 0 \\
\frac{1}{2} & 0 & \frac{3}{2}
}
$
has three distinct eigenvalues and one of its eigenvectors is 
$
\myvec{ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 }.
$
Which one of the following can be another eigenvector of $\vec{A}$?
\hfill(EE 2017)
\begin{enumerate}
\begin{multicols}{4}
\item $\myvec{ 0 \\ 0 \\ -1 }$
\item $\myvec{ -1 \\ 0 \\ 0 }$
\item $\myvec{ 0 \\ 1 \\ -1 }$
\item $\myvec{ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 }$
\end{multicols}
\end{enumerate}
